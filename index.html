  • What are esports?

    Esports is a form of competition using video games. Esports often takes the form of organised, multiplayer video game competitions, particularly between professional players, individually or as teams.

  • Popular Titles

    Popular esport franchises include League of Legends, Dota, Counter-Strike, Valorant,Rainbow Six Siege

  • Tournaments

    The tournament may be part of a larger gathering, such as Dreamhack, or the competition may be the entirety of the event. Esport competitions have also become a popular feature at gaming and multi-genre conventions Governing bodies

  • Governing bodies

    The International Esports Federation (IESF) was one of the first such bodies. The IESF has managed annual Esport World Championships for teams from its member countries across multiple games.

Global Esports Federation

Anticipating an even-greater surge in its growth and eyeing the acceleration of esports as a key driver of the economy worldwide, the Global Esports Federation announces the appointment of Paul J. Foster as Chief Executive Officer. Foster will assume the role as GEF’s first CEO. Since last April, he has served as Chief Operating Officer. He has been instrumental in overseeing the Singapore-based organization’s advance since worldwide launch in December 2019 – the GEF now counting more than 80 Member Federations and well over 100 partnerships.
